Possible Commands for KIX

Version: 2.0.6 (2017-01-04)

KIX

Aggregation of chained time series with annual overlap method
Formula: [Name=]KIX,i1,[w1,]+/-,i2,[w2,]...,refyr
Examples:
1. KIX,i1,+,i2,2005
The shortest version of the KIX command. The programm will do an additive aggregation of the index series i1 and i2 with the referenz year 2005.
As weights for the aggregation it will use the weight series w1 for i1 and w2 for i2.
2. KIX,i1,w3,+,i2,w1,2005
The programm will do an additive aggregation of the index series i1 and i2 with the referenz year 2005.
As weights for the aggregation it will use the weight series w3 for i1 and w1 for i2.
3. KIX,i1,+,i2,-,i3,2010
The programm will do an additive aggregation of the index series i1 and i2 and an subtractive aggregation of the index series i3 with the referenz year 2010.
As weights for the aggregation it will use the weight series w1 for i1, w2 for i2 and w3 for i3.
4. Example4=KIX,i1,+,i2,-,i3,2010
The same as Example 3 but the programm will name the resulting time series Example4.

WBG

Calculates the growth rate contribution of a chained time series (iContr) to another chained time series (iTotal) lagged by #ofLags.
The number of lags must be between 1 and the periodicity of the time series. Chaining and unchaining is done with the annual overlap method.
Formula: [Name=]WBG,iContr,[wContr,]+/-,iTotal,[wTotal,]#ofLags
Examples:
1. WBG,i1,+,i2,2
The shortest version of the WBG command. The programm will calculate the growth rate contribution of the index series i1 to i2 lagged by 2 periods.
As weights for the calculation it will use the weight series w1 for i1 and w2 for i2.
2. WBG,i1,w3,+,i2,w1,2
The programm will calculate the growth rate contribution of the index series i1 to i2 lagged by 2 periods.
As weights for the calculation it will use the weight series w3 for i1 and w1 for i2.
3. Example3=WBG,i1,w3,+,i2,w1,2
The same as Example 2 but the programm will name the resulting time series Example3.

Examples:
1. UNC,i1
The programm will unchain the index series i1.
2. UNC,w1
The programm will unchain the weight series w1.
3. Example3=UNC,i1
The same as Example 1 but the programm will name the resulting time series Example3.

CHA

Chain a time series (i1) with the annual overlap method.
Formula: [Name=]CHA,i1[,refyr]
Examples:
1. CHA,i1
The programm will chain the index series i1. The resulting time series is normalized on the first year.
2. CHA,w1
The programm will chain the weight series w1. The resulting time series is normalized on the first year.
3. CHA,i1,2005
The programm will chain the index series i1. The resulting time series is normalized on 2005.
4. Example4=CHA,i1
The same as Example 1 but the programm will name the resulting time series Example4.

FBI

Does a weighted aggregation of the specified time series
Formula: [Name=]FBI,i1,w1/number,+/-,i2,w2/number,...
Number format: ±#.#E±# e.g. 2.5E-2 Examples:
1. FBI,i1,w1,+,i2,w2
The programm will add i1 and i2 and use w1 as weight for i1 and w2 for i2.
2. FBI,i1,1,+,i2,2
The programm will add i1 and i2 and use 1 as weight for i1 and 2 for i2.
3. FBI,i1,2.5,+,i2,w2
The programm will add i1 and i2 and use 2.5 as weight for i1 and w2 for i2.
4. Example4=FBI,i1,w1,+,i2,w2
The same as Example 1 but the programm will name the resulting time series Example4.
